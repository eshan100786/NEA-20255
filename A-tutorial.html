Teacher Mode Tutorial

This mode is designed to assist teachers in explaining math concepts to students in a classroom setting. Here's how it works:

Select a Topic: Begin by selecting a topic from the available options: multiplication, algebra, geometry, or quadratics. Each topic will present a structured question format to help facilitate teaching.

Canvas for Demonstrations: The bottom half of the screen features a canvas where you can visually demonstrate how to solve a question to your students. This can be particularly helpful for illustrating problem-solving steps or demonstrating mathematical concepts.

Topic Change: On the left side of the screen, you'll find a "Topic Change" button. This allows you to switch between different topics at any time, providing flexibility in lesson planning and instruction.

This mode empowers teachers to engage students effectively by providing structured questions, interactive demonstrations, and flexibility in topic selection. It's a valuable tool for enhancing math instruction in the classroom.

Student Mode Tutorial

In Student Mode, you'll have the opportunity to test your math skills and track your progress. Here's how it works:

Enter Your Name: Start by entering your name in the provided field. This will personalize your experience and allow you to track your performance.

Pick a Topic: Choose a topic from the available options. Each topic contains three questions tailored to that specific area of math.

Answer Questions: Once you've selected a topic, you'll be presented with three questions related to that topic. Use your math skills to solve each question to the best of your ability.

Track Your Score: Your score will be displayed on the left side of the screen at all times. As you answer questions correctly, your score will increase. This score will determine your grade at the end of the session.

Workout Area: Below the question section, there's a canvas where you can work out your solutions or use it to jot down any notes. Alternatively, you can also work out in your book.

Finish and Grade: Once you've completed the questions for a topic, instead of moving to the next topic, you'll press "Finish". Your grade will be determined based on the number of questions you've answered correctly. Grades range from A to F.
